Where Music Comes to Life

Andrew Potts' private music studio offers piano and voice lessons for all ages and skill levels in the New Philadelphia/Dover Ohio area. If you're interested in starting lessons for you or your child, feel free to contact an instructor and set up a visit!

Studio Offerings

Piano and Voice lessons are offered throughout the year to best fit the needs of each individual student and family. A typical year of lessons will follow the regular academic calendar with 15 weeks of lessons per semester.

Lesson duration will differ depending on the student. Half-hour lessons are fast-paced and geared toward beginners and youngsters as a way to keep them engaged and actively learning. Forty-Five minute lessons are concentrated but more relaxed and allow for more in-depth study. Sixty minute lessons are for the advanced students who have more literature to study. The appropriate length of lesson will be suggested by the instructor at the initial meeting.

Tuition

Tuition for each semester is billed monthly and will be payable via PayPal or personal check. A monthly invoice will include 3-4 lessons (depending on holidays) and occasional methods books (which range from $6-12 each, depending on the book).

Meet the Instructor

Andrew Potts is a graduate of the University of Mount Union (2010) and holds his Bachelor of Music Performance in Piano Performance degree with secondary studies in Voice and Harpsichord. During his time at UMU, he received many high-value scholarships and awards, including the Music Proficiency Scholarship, Hartshorn Academic Award, Sophomore Choral Leadership Award, and the Agnes Flowers Music Scholarship. He was an active member of various collaborative ensembles and performing groups.

Mr. Potts is currently working in the New Philadelphia High School Choir Department serving as the accompanist for their five choirs, including the Delphian Chorale (of which he is an alum). Several choral arrangements written by Mr. Potts have been heard by the community throughout performances by the choirs in recent years.

In his free time, Andrew is also active in the theatre world, directing and acting in various stage plays and musicals. He is currently the director of the NPHS Drama Department, making the creative decisions and educating students about the art of theatre.
